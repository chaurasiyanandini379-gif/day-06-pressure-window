# FlashCart Incident Scenario Package

## Startup Context

FlashCart runs high-demand limited product drops.

Previous drops already exposed checkout weakness. Day 5 focused on reviewing an AI-generated checkout fix. Day 6 puts you inside a live drop where pressure has arrived.

## Product Drop Stakes

During the drop:

- thousands of customers hit checkout
- inventory is limited
- payment gateway events may lag or duplicate
- support volume rises fast
- public screenshots spread quickly
- founder wants to preserve revenue

## Customer Impact

Customers may see:

- payment succeeded but no order confirmation
- duplicate charges
- refund uncertainty
- unavailable item after payment
- vague support responses

Customer-money impact is the most dangerous trust risk.

## Founder Pressure

Founder message:

> Revenue is live. Support is exploding. You cannot fix everything. Protect what matters first.

The founder is not a villain. They are trying to keep the business alive while pressure rises.

## Support Overload

Support agents need clear guidance:

- what to tell paid customers
- when refund guidance is safe
- whether checkout is paused, throttled, or live
- whether public status messaging exists

Silence becomes its own incident.

## Revenue Pressure

Stopping checkout protects customers but damages the launch.

Keeping checkout live preserves revenue but may increase payment/order harm.

There is no perfect choice.

## Trust Implications

Strong incident command means:

- decide what gets protected first
- communicate what is uncertain
- contain blast radius
- accept visible tradeoffs
- create a next action plan
