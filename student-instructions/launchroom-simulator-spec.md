# LaunchRoom Simulator Specification

## Product

FlashCart - Drop Incident Command

## Purpose

Create realistic pressure without live founder chaos.

The simulator tests constrained prioritization, not multitasking endurance.

## Event Flow

1. Briefing and student identity capture
2. 10-minute pressure window
3. War-room messages reveal over time
4. Metrics degrade as incident progresses
5. Decision prompts appear at staged moments
6. Choices alter metrics and stakeholder reactions
7. Timeouts apply default consequences
8. One AI pressure event tests bounded AI judgment
9. Final report is generated

## Decision Points

Decision points cover:

- traffic containment
- refund handling
- engineering priority
- founder escalation
- support containment
- AI recommendation judgment
- customer communication
- rollback/hotfix/isolation
- final containment line

## Consequence Logic

Each decision affects:

- active users
- success rate
- failed payments
- duplicate charges
- webhook lag
- inventory mismatch
- refund requests
- support tickets
- error rate
- founder pressure
- support pressure

Good outcomes are not all possible at once.

## Timeout Behavior

If no decision is made, the simulator applies a default consequence.

Timeouts are not instant failure. They are evidence of weak command presence when repeated.

## AI Event Injection

One bounded AI pressure event appears during the incident:

> AI suggests rollback checkout immediately and auto-refund suspected duplicates.

Student must choose:

- trust AI
- modify AI
- ignore AI

Expected lesson:

> Under pressure, AI can suggest a plausible move, but tradeoff ownership still belongs to the engineer.

## End-State Reporting

The final report includes:

- completion status
- decision timeline
- consequence summary
- incident prioritization matrix
- stakeholder incident brief
- performance signals
- leadership signals
- portfolio summary

Founder evaluation must combine the generated report with the Commander Decision Brief, video, and viva.
